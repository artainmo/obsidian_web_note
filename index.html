ADHD stimulants give effortful behavior and sustained attention to help work by increasing tonic dopamine. But they can worsen sensory-gating and anxiety while those can affect focus and thus the ability to work. They have ambiguous effects on phasic dopamine, usually lowering it at therapeutic ADHD doses but sometimes increasing it at high doses. Phasic dopamine increases motivation to allow work. A minimal amount of anxiety can also help motivation, some lack of sensory-gating could improve creativity, and some lack of sustained attention too, but phasic dopamine also. Tipepidine will improve effortful behavior and sustained attention too, even if to a lesser extent maybe compared to higher doses of ADHD stimulants, but tipepidine improves phasic dopamine, sensory-gating, anxiety, while ADHD stimulants do not. Thus, tipepidine can be very useful when ADHD stimulants are not. But what works best for someone depends either way on their baseline neurochemistry since someone may be too anxious or not enough, same with creativity and sensory-gating, or sometimes too much motivation/phasic-dopamine even can be negative at times due to creating distractibility for more fun tasks.
