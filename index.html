How to study at university

Study methods

  • Try to understand the big pictures, understand the general concepts instead of memorising details.
    • The Feynman technique can help with this, which consists of explaining complicated concepts in simple words.
  • Use as memorisation techniques spaced repetition and active recall with an app called Anki instead of rereading, highlighting and summarising.
    • Spaced repetition 
      • By introducing time intervals between study session you can remember more which is a proven phenomenon called the spacing effect.
    • Active recall
      • Consists of retrieving information from own brain instead of trying to encode external information in brain, basically frequent testing after initial learning.
      • Practice testing consists of frequent testing/quizzing about study concepts and comes down to the same as active recall.
      • Active recall is more difficult than rereading but is proven to be superior for revision.
    • Anki: https://apps.ankiweb.net
      • Anki is a flashcard app that uses an algorithm built around active recall and spaced repetition.
      • Flashcards are small note cards used for testing through practiced information retrieval.
      • Avoid creating excessive flashcards, first understand the big picture, but at the same time advice exists of directly creating flashcards instead of taking notes.
      • More advanced tips on Anki: https://medshamim.com/medicine
  • Certain topics can best be visualised by diagram drawings, repeating as an exercise the diagram drawing yourself is highly helpful.
  • For subjects consisting of exercises such as math or physics, practice a wide variety of exercises to be exposed to different cases. 
  • To learn complicated concepts, it is important to understand them. Explaining them to others is great to understand them and remember them.
  • Study time and breaks
    • Pomodoro technique may not be ideal as it takes +-20min to enter deep focus. However deep focus is not always of importance, it would be for non-linear theory and practicing but less for linear memorisation.
    • One ultradian cycle consists of increasing arousal, followed by decreasing arousal during 90min, followed by 20min of low arousal. Based on this, studying 90min with 20min breaks would be ideal. However arousal stemming from stress, maybe fasting and stimulants can override this ultradian cycle and thus allow for longer periods of focus, for example two 4hour sessions. 
    • Breaks with low arousal and where the mind is empty are important for memory consolidation, even breaks lasting seconds while studying. For longer breaks lack of new incoming information, non-sleep-deep-rest or even a nap would be ideal, also rewarding oneself can help maintain motivation.
    • Chronotypes can influence personal moment of the day with highest attention, for some this occurs in morning others in evening, usually everybody is fatigued during the afternoon and after meals.
      • Only stimulants or fasting can increase alertness during the afternoon. Taking the daily coffee right before noon may help. Else, to be very selective, methylliberine can be taken after lunch. For other pharmacological tools I won’t write those here.
    • Overall timings should mainly be determined by personal preferences.

Lectures

  • Pre-read lectures it will improve the return from a lecture.
  • Take slides as a summary and add notes to those while attending the associated class or directly write flashcards or simply take notes on blank paper.
    • Use this app to write on slides from mac?: https://support.apple.com/guide/mac-help/mark-up-files-mchl1fd88863/mac

Study content

  • Slides plus notes/flashcards should be main study content.
  • Avoid spending too much time studying unnecessary details in the syllabus, they are only useful in the clarification of certain details or understanding of certain topics. 
  • Instead of syllabus do not hesitate to use google, YouTube, wikipedia for clarifications or even to pre-read lectures.

Other advice

  • Study selectively what will be asked on exams, learn what should be studied from lectures.
  • Have a routine and study in library to avoid distraction opportunities found in home, obviously avoid distractions such as phone, mails or other notification-based distractions.
  • Become friend with same and older years and ask them to share study resources and other infos.

Learn and set everything up step by step

  • Anki method
    • We can download anki on her computer and start using it for the exercise.
  • Obsidian
    • Application to easily take notes and find them back, this is also a long-term investment to have a knowledge base where you can easily find information back.
    • Create first note by copying (school, study, tips)
    • Create second note by copying Obsidian
      • Learn to use tags, name files based on tags, and write in markdown.
      • Customize the ‘This Vault’ section. For university you can use tags about the school year, subject, lesson number, and the note title can be based just on that.
    • Look at obsidian_to_anki plugins
    • One day you can contemplate buying Obsidian Copilot if vault is large enough.
